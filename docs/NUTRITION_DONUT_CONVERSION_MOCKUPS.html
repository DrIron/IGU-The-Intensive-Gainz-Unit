4 · PhaseSummaryCard (NU6 — the shareable PNG)

Keep the ribbon src/components/nutrition/PhaseSummaryCard.tsx → PhaseSummaryReport

Three reasons the donut is the wrong call here, and they compound.
(1) It fights the hero. This card exists to say one thing — the result, −4.2 kg, deliberately neutral text-foreground under the NU6 honesty contract. A second, brightly-coloured focal point halves the impact of the only number that matters. "Average macros" is footnote context, not the subject.
(2) Export risk. This is the one artifact that leaves the app, rasterised by html-to-image. The ribbon paints its tokens as backgroundColor on divs; the donut paints the same tokens as an SVG stroke attribute — a different code path through the serialiser. A silent colour-drop here ships a grey donut to the client's Instagram.
(3) Nothing is gained. The legend's grams-and-% upgrade is the donut's whole argument, and on a share card the % of calories from fat is not what anyone is sharing.

What Part IV must now say

Part IV currently reads: "The ribbon and standalone donut are retired into it." That sentence is false after this ruling, and leaving it in invites the next build session to "finish the job" and convert the two surfaces that were deliberately spared. Replace it with the rule below.

The rule. Every surface that presents calories + macros as its subject renders NutritionSummary. The MacroDistributionRibbon survives in exactly two roles, and adding a third is a decision, not a reflex:
  (a) a compact notification where the split is supporting context and a donut would outweigh the message (NutritionProgress's adjustment notice);
  (b) the NU6 share card, where a second focal point would compete with the result number the card exists to deliver, and where SVG strokes carry avoidable html-to-image export risk.

The standalone MacroDonut import is retired — it survives only as NutritionSummary's internal ring. No surface imports it directly.
