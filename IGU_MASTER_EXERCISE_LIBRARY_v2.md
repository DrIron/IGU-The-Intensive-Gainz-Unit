**21 Sections | ~45 Subdivisions | ~90 Movements | ~360 Exercises**

**Equipment Key:**
- **BB** = Barbell / EZ Bar
- **DB** = Dumbbell
- **M** = Machine (plate-loaded or selectorized)
- **C-FT** = Cable -- Functional Trainer (fixed pillar columns, pulleys slide up/down)
- **C-AA** = Cable -- Adjustable Arms (arms swing to angle)
- **C-FS** = Cable -- Fixed Seated (seated cable station with fixed angle)
- **BW** = Bodyweight / Bands
- **KB** = Kettlebell

**Resistance Profile Acronyms (in exercise names):**
- **(L)** = Lengthened -- Peak tension at stretched position
- **(M)** = Mid-range -- Peak tension in middle of ROM
- **(S)** = Shortened -- Peak tension at contracted position

# 1. CHEST (Pectoralis Major)

## 1.1 Clavicular Head (Upper)

**Function:** Shoulder flexion + horizontal adduction at a low-to-high angle. Humerus directed toward the inner third of the clavicle.

### Movement 1: Press

> **Execution:**
> - Bring both humeri toward the inner third of the clavicle
> - Full elbow extension during the concentric to allow the humerus to reach closer to the clavicular origin
> - Control the eccentric — lower until the elbow passes just below shoulder level
> - Slight scapular retraction during the eccentric to increase lengthened range of motion
> - Focus on your humerus moving rather than your hands — the pectoral muscle acts on the humerus, not the hand

# 2. SHOULDERS (Deltoids)

## 2.1 Anterior Deltoid

**Function:** Shoulder flexion, horizontal adduction, internal rotation. Training focus: push/pull the humerus upward toward the lateral third of the clavicle.

### Movement 1: Press

> **Execution:**
> - Press both humeri upward and in
> - Elbows slightly brought in, in close proximity to the scapular plane — wherever comfortable
> - Full elbow extension during the concentric to allow the humerus to reach full overhead position
> - Allow natural scapular upward rotation as the arms go overhead — do not restrict it
> - Allow scapular retraction during the eccentric
> - Control the eccentric — lower until the elbow passes just below shoulder level
> - Focus on the humerus moving — not the hands

## 2.3 Posterior Deltoid

**Function:** Shoulder extension, horizontal abduction, external rotation. Fiber angle approximately perpendicular to scapular spine (~45 degrees).

### Movement 1: Reverse Fly

> **Execution:**
> - Pull both humeri back and apart at a comfortable angle where elbows are below shoulder level
> - Focus on shoulder extension — pull the humerus back as much as possible and as if slightly pulling the humeri in toward each other at the end of the motion when targeting a full contraction
> - Focus on the humerus moving — not the hands
> - Allow natural scapular retraction throughout the movement
> - The concentric ends as the humerus reaches approximately in line with the torso
> - Control the eccentric — allow the humeri to travel forward under control
> - Careful not to initiate with the mid-back — the movement starts from the humerus

# 5. ELBOW FLEXORS

## 5.1 Biceps -- Long Head

**Function:** Elbow flexion, forearm supination. Long head crosses shoulder joint. Involves humeral external rotation. Shoulder position determines resistance profile: extended = lengthened, flexed = shortened.

### Movement 1: Curl (shoulder extended -- lengthened)

> **Execution:**
> - Slightly tense the back to keep the shoulder in place
> - Keep the shoulder in its starting extended position — minor natural movement is fine, but do not let the elbow drift forward during the curl
> - Pull the forearm toward the humerus — elbow flexion
> - Maintain a supinated wrist position throughout — do not allow the wrist to fall into extension during the movement
> - Control the eccentric — allow full elbow extension under control
